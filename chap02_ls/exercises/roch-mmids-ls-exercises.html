Answer and justification for E2.2.1: Yes, \(U\) is a linear subspace of \(\mathbb{R}^3\). Let \(u_1 = (x_1, y_1, z_1), u_2 = (x_2, y_2, z_2) \in U\) and \(\alpha \in \mathbb{R}\). Then
